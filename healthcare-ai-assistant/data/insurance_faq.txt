Insurance and Billing FAQ

What insurance plans do you accept?
We accept most major insurance plans including Medicare, Medicaid, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, and Humana. Patients should verify their specific plan coverage before scheduling appointments.

Do you offer payment plans?
Yes, we offer interest-free payment plans for balances over $200. Patients can set up automatic monthly payments through the patient portal or at the front desk. A $25 down payment is required to establish a payment plan.

What are the co-pay amounts?
Co-pay amounts vary by insurance plan and visit type. Typical co-pays are $25 for primary care visits, $50 for specialist visits, and $75 for urgent care visits. Patients should check with their insurance provider for specific amounts.

How do I submit an insurance claim?
Insurance claims are submitted electronically by our billing department after each visit. Patients do not need to submit claims themselves. If manual submission is required, claim forms are available at the front desk or on the patient portal.

What is the self-pay discount?
Patients without insurance or who choose not to use insurance receive a 20% discount on all services. Self-pay rates must be arranged before the appointment and payment is expected at the time of service.

Are lab tests covered by insurance?
Most preventive lab tests are covered at 100% under the Affordable Care Act. Diagnostic lab tests may require co-pay or coinsurance depending on the specific insurance plan. Patients should check with their insurance provider for coverage details.

How long does insurance processing take?
Insurance claims are typically processed within 14-30 business days. If a claim is denied, patients will receive an explanation of benefits (EOB) from their insurance company. Our billing department can assist with appeals and resubmissions.

Do you accept out-of-network insurance?
We are in-network providers for the insurance plans listed above. Out-of-network patients may be eligible for out-of-network benefits depending on their plan. Patients should verify coverage with their insurance provider before scheduling.

What documentation do I need for insurance?
Patients should bring their insurance card, photo ID, and any required referral or prior authorization documents to each appointment. Updated insurance information should be provided annually or when coverage changes.

How do I dispute a bill?
Billing disputes should be directed to the billing department within 60 days of receiving the statement. Patients can contact billing by phone, email, or through the patient portal. All disputes are reviewed and resolved within 30 business days.
