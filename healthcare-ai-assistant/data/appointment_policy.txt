Appointment Scheduling Policy

Patients may schedule appointments online through the patient portal, by phone, or in person at the front desk. Online scheduling is available 24/7 for routine appointments.

Cardiology appointments are available Monday to Friday from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Same-day cardiology appointments are available for urgent referrals only.

Appointment cancellations require 24-hour notice. Cancellations made less than 24 hours before the scheduled time may result in a $50 cancellation fee.

New patients should arrive 30 minutes before their scheduled appointment time to complete registration paperwork and provide insurance information.

Appointment durations vary by type: routine check-ups are scheduled for 30 minutes, specialist consultations for 45 minutes, and comprehensive annual exams for 60 minutes.

Patients who arrive more than 15 minutes late may be asked to reschedule their appointment. This policy ensures timely care for all patients.

Follow-up appointments should be scheduled before leaving the office. Patients who need to schedule follow-ups after hours can do so through the patient portal or by calling the scheduling line.

Group appointments and wellness classes are available for patients with chronic conditions such as diabetes, hypertension, and heart disease. These sessions provide education and peer support.

Pre-operative appointments must be completed within 30 days before the scheduled surgery. These appointments include lab work, imaging review, and anesthesia consultation.

Telehealth appointments follow the same scheduling procedures as in-person visits. Patients should indicate their preference for telehealth when booking.

Wait times may vary based on provider availability and patient volume. Patients will be notified of any significant delays and given the option to reschedule.

Appointment reminders are sent via text message, email, or phone call 48 hours before the scheduled time. Patients are responsible for updating their contact information in the patient portal.
