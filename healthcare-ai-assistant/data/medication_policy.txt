Medication Management Policy

Prescription refills require 48-72 hours for processing. Patients should request refills at least one week before running out of medication. Emergency refills may be available on a case-by-case basis.

Controlled substance prescriptions follow strict DEA regulations. Opioids, benzodiazepines, stimulants, and other Schedule II-V medications require in-person evaluation every 90 days. No early refills are permitted for controlled substances.

Medication refills during telehealth visits are limited to chronic medications previously prescribed by our providers. New medication requests require an in-person evaluation unless the patient has an established relationship with the provider.

Mail-order pharmacy services are available for maintenance medications. Prescriptions can be sent directly to the patient's home with free standard shipping. Express shipping is available for an additional $10 fee.

Generic medications are prescribed when available and clinically appropriate. Patients may request brand-name medications but may be responsible for additional costs. Our pharmacy team works with insurance companies to minimize out-of-pocket expenses.

Medication prior authorizations are processed within 3-5 business days. Our clinical staff coordinates with insurance companies to obtain approvals for medications requiring prior authorization. Patients are notified when approvals are received or denied.

Patient education on medication usage is provided at each prescription. Pharmacists are available for comprehensive medication reviews and counseling. Patients are encouraged to ask questions about dosing, side effects, and drug interactions.

Prescription transfers from other providers require verification of medical history. Patients should provide the name and contact information of their previous provider. Transfer prescriptions are limited to 90 days unless medical records are received.

Medication samples are available for select medications to help patients during insurance transitions or financial difficulties. Sample medications are provided at no cost and are limited to a 30-day supply.

High-risk medications including anticoagulants, insulin, and immunosuppressants require additional monitoring. Patients on these medications have more frequent follow-up appointments and lab work to ensure safety and efficacy.

Medication reconciliation is performed at each visit to ensure accuracy and identify potential interactions. Patients should bring all current medications including over-the-counter drugs and supplements to each appointment.

Refill denials are reviewed by the prescribing provider. If a refill is denied, patients will be contacted to schedule an appointment for reevaluation. Documentation of the denial and reason is maintained in the patient's medical record.
