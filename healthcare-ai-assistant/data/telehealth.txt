Telehealth Consultation Policy

Telehealth consultations are available Monday to Friday from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Weekend telehealth services are available for urgent care only from 9:00 AM to 2:00 PM.

Patients may request medication refills during telehealth consultations. Refills for chronic medications such as blood pressure medications, diabetes medications, and thyroid medications can be processed during virtual visits.

Controlled substances require in-person evaluation. Telehealth prescriptions for controlled substances such as opioids, benzodiazepines, and stimulants are not permitted without an initial in-person consultation.

Telehealth visits are covered by most insurance plans including Medicare and Medicaid. Patients should verify coverage with their insurance provider before scheduling.

Technical requirements for telehealth include a stable internet connection, a device with camera and microphone capability, and a quiet private location. Patients must be physically located within the state where their provider is licensed.

Telehealth consultations are recorded for quality assurance and medical record purposes. Patients will be informed of recording before the session begins and must provide consent.

Emergency situations should not be handled through telehealth. Patients experiencing chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, or other emergencies should call 911 or visit the nearest emergency department.

Follow-up appointments after surgery or procedures may be conducted via telehealth at the discretion of the treating physician. Post-operative wound checks that require physical examination must be done in person.

Mental health services including therapy and psychiatric evaluations are available through telehealth. Initial psychiatric evaluations for new patients require an in-person visit before telehealth sessions can be scheduled.

Pediatric telehealth consultations are available for patients aged 2 and above. Children under 2 require in-person visits for all medical concerns.
